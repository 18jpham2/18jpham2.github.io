Facts:
  • Born in New York, 1945
  • Graduated from Bronx High School of Science in 1962
  • Received a Bachelor in electrical engineering from New York University in 1966
  • Received a Master in 1967 and a Ph. D. in 1969 from Stanford
  • Left IBM to teach in Electrical Engineering at MIT in 1969
  • Published New Directions in Cryptography in 1976
  • Played a leading role in the late 1970s' and early 1980s' first ''crypto war''
  • Worked with Soviet scientists in the 1980s to reduce nuclear weapons
  • Won the IEEE Centennial Medal in 1984
  • Won the Electronic Frontier Foundation Pioneer Award in 1994
  • Became professor emeritus in electrical engineering at Stanford University in 1996
  • Was elected to the National Academy of Engineering in 2002
  • Won the ACM Turing Award (Nobel Prize of Computer Science) in 2015
